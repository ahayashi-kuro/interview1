About HOKKAIDO

I would like to introduce you to Hokkaido where I grew up. Hokkaido, located in the northernmost part of Japan, is known for its stunning natural beauty, including mountains, national parks, and hot springs, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The region boasts world-class ski resorts like Niseko, abundant dairy products such as rich milk and ice cream, and fresh seafood, especially crab and salmon. Hokkaido is also home to the indigenous Ainu people, whose unique culture and traditions enrich the island's identity. Additionally, the vibrant food scene, highlighted by local specialties like ramen, and seasonal festivals, including the famous Sapporo Snow Festival, further enhance its appeal to visitors.
